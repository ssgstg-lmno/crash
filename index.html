How people under 18 get less choice, and less political choice

Unless specifically stated, none of the theoretical stories are true, but could happen.

"The right of citizens of the United States, who are eighteen years of age or older, to vote shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or by any State on account of age." - The 26th amendment. This amendment was the one which gave everyone over 18 the right to vote. However, most people under 18 still want to make changes, and get ignored by most of the population, since they're "too young," or "immature". People under 18 still have rights, but everyone seems to forget that.

Now imagine this. You are a student in 10th grade, and your 16th birthday is coming up. Your school caught some people sneaking into the bathroom to skip class, so they do the "reasonable" thing, and start to track every single place that students go, limiting how many times they leave the classroom, and for how long. Not outside of school, but still, this seems illegal, and an invasion of privacy. You check in with the principal, and they say that it's fine, it's not like they are tracking people. You start telling people about it, but no one cares, since it is, "just a hall pass," but something doesn't seem right to you. You find an news report about amazon doing the same thing, tracking where and when people go, and being off of task for to long gets you fired. This seems like the same thing, except that amazon got sued. Some students are getting suspended, or even expelled because they took to long in the bathroom.
